TAMIL MOVIE OF THE YEAR

From the outset, Maharaja distinguished itself by tackling a seemingly simple premise — a man filing a complaint about a missing object called “Lakshmi” — yet gradually revealing layered emotional and moral undercurrents. The script by Nithilan Saminathan (who also directed) subverts expectations. Many reviews point to how the film defies straightforward revenge‐thriller formulas through its non‐linear narrative, its twisting revelations and the way it reframes characters’ motivations. This writerly ambition is one of the key reasons the film has been recognized and awarded — it shows craft, originality, and willingness to go beyond familiar tropes. One award milestone: Maharaja won the Best Director award at the Indian Film Festival of Melbourne, underscoring the global appreciation of Saminathan’s direction. Puthiyathalaimurai That international acknowledgement affirms that the film’s technique and thematic ambitions resonated beyond regional cinema. 1. Screenplay & narrative structure The screenplay plays a major role. Critics praise it for being “brilliant” and for being a “visual puzzle” that keeps you invested. The Times of India It doesn’t rely on overt exposition; instead it gradually unfolds the truth, keeps the audience guessing, and then delivers emotional catharsis. As one essay observed, for example: “What makes the director a freaking genius is the frame … the whole story unfolds just because … The chain is thrown to him at the end of the film…” Medium This kind of careful ordering and layering of story beats gives the film a structural sophistication not always seen in mainstream Tamil thrills. The fact that prominent industry figures like Venkat Prabhu publicly praised the screenplay (“brilliant screenplay!”) adds weight to this. At the heart of the film is lead actor Vijay Sethupathi, whose portrayal of the titular “Maharaja” (a barber turned avenger/father) is frequently cited as one of his career-best turns. One article states: “Vijay Sethupathi has delivered one of the best films in Tamil cinema history … versatile actor … outstanding performance.”
